Design Implications

Aesthetic considerations are the way that my character looks
visually overall. This is important because I addressed my
character’s aesthetics by making them distinctive by keeping
to a theme. This theming should be around the color blue and have an
air theme. This theming should make my character stand
out more and become more recognisable.

Functionality ensures that the character works properly
within the game. This is important because my character
working is one of the most important aspects of this
project.I addressed the functionality of my character by
taking into account of aniamtions that my character will
need to be able to do. These consist of running animations,
jumping animations, gun pullout animations as well as
Firing animations.

Usability affects how easily players can understand and
interact with my character. This is important because to
make a game that people want to play it has to be usable
Players can easily use and understand my character because
I am taking into consideration simplifing aspects of
the character to make the important aspects of the
stand out more. This will direct Players attention to the
most important design parts of the character. I will also
make sure that the controls for my character are intuative

Intellectual property protects original ideas from being
copied. It’s important because as I do not want my work and
character being used for things that I do not want them
to be used for. To ensure my character is original, I must make
my character distinctive and recognisable so that it is obvious that they
are my character and not someone elses.

Development Process

Turnaround

I drew a character turnaround for my character. This was a quick sketch
mostly to judge proportions for my 3d model. By using the 8 heads template I
can get the proportions to make my character look lanky and tall.
To complete this step, first I got reference images, then Imagined in my head
what this character should look like. After this I drew the sketch using
proportions
I have made decisions such as making him tall, as I think this fits his character
as he is a steampunk/fantasy engineer who is highly skilled with firearms. This
would mean that he would not have much time to train with other weapons.
Some other key design elements were the gas mask and goggles. This is to
add to the steampunk aesthetic and tell a story about the character. His skin
is light blue (Although not seeable from the turnaround) because he is an air
genasi (a Person with wind elemental energy in them). Keeping with this
theme I have made his robes a darker blue to fit into this color theme.

This yeilded mixed results as there were issues with the allignment.
The drawings as well were subpar and did not capture the concept I was trying to recreate
An additional way I tested this turnaround, is that I asked
for feedback from my peers. They echoed my thoughts
about the issue with the allignement and suggested that I used mechanisms such as a
function on one of the printers to upload my image directly
from the paper copy to my drive, this meant that I did not have to concern
Myself with Photo allignement.
Positive feedback I got from my peers, is that
they liked the concept and thoguht the drawing, while not perfect was alright

Based on the testing and feedback, I decided that my main issue was allignement
So I sought out to fix that. To do that, I used the upload function the printer to accuratly
upload my image to the computer without having any issues
The Reason that alligment was vital was that it meant that if it was not

My Turnaround is now more effective due to these changes

Modelling

I modelled the base of my character.
This model contained the body, head, arms and legs of the model.
I used my previous reference to get a basic shape of the
body, and then continued to model it in more detail
for the head, I have made the head scale in with the rest of the relatively slim body; this is a
key part of my character, and makes it recognisable. I have also carefully
made places for other components of the characters head, redially avaliable
to be used
For the body, I have made this character purposefully quite slim as that is his general
build. I mirrored this approach in the arms and legs making him proportionate to the rest of the body
I have tried to fit the vibe of a steampunk engineer into this model and have thought about
what who he is makes him look like.
This thinking has guided my design and modelling process

I tested my design by comparing it to my reference
and it turned out relatively similar to what I imagined for the reference.
I also as earlier asked my peers for feedback. While they liked the general
model, they thought that there were improvements to be made to the head.
Some positive feedback that came from my peers was that they liked the level
of detail on the rest of the body

Based on my testing and feedback, I decided that the head was the biggest issue.
So got to work and I got rid of the bump and weird angle
on top of the head,as well as making the face less wide for more realism
I made these adjustments to make the head more realistic
and natural rather than the alien face shape I had before
The changes additionally conformed to my character more.
I think this is a better representation of my idea for this character
as it is more natural and human. This makes the model stronger and meets my
specifications and requirements more accuratly.

Animation

I animated my model. By using the animation function in Blender.
To animate the model, first I had to rig it.
Rigging is a process which requires putting an armature
Which is kind of like a skeleton for the model. In inside
the model and having them connected through a process called parenting.
After I did this I was free to animate my model.
My first project for this was to get an idle animation.
For this I made the model bop up and down.

I tested my animation by running it and seeing how it worked.
It was functional, however I felt that the movements were over exagerated.
When I asked some of my peers about the animation, they suggested adding in arm movements
and body movements that were not only vertical
such as the character leaning over slightly to make it seem more natural
